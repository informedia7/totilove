Safety Tips

Your safety is our top priority. Please follow these guidelines to stay safe when interacting online and meeting in person.

Back to Home

Protect Your Personal Information

Never share sensitive personal information with other users, especially in early conversations.

This includes:

  • Full name
  • Home address
  • Workplace details
  • Financial information
  • Identity documents

Use the in-app messaging system until you feel comfortable and trust the other person.

Reminder: No home address • No bank details • No ID documents

Video Call Before Meeting

Before meeting in person, we strongly recommend having a video call first.

This helps:

  • confirm identity
  • build trust
  • reduce risk of impersonation or scams

Safe First Meetings

When meeting someone for the first time:

  • Meet in a public, well-populated place (e.g. café, restaurant, park)
  • Tell a friend or family member your plans (who, where, and when)
  • Arrange your own transport and avoid accepting rides from strangers
  • Keep your phone charged and accessible at all times

Recognise Red Flags

Be cautious if you notice:

  • Requests for money, gifts, or financial assistance (romance scams)
  • Refusal to video call after extended chatting
  • Inconsistent or unrealistic personal stories
  • Pressure to move conversations off the platform (e.g. WhatsApp, Telegram)
  • Excessive flattery or fast emotional attachment

Report & Block

If something feels suspicious or unsafe, trust your instincts.

You can:

  • Use the Report button on profiles or messages
  • Block users instantly from their profile page

All reports are reviewed by our moderation team as quickly as reasonably possible.

Emergency Resources

If you are in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services.

For support related to online abuse or image misuse, you may also refer to:

  • StopNCII.org
  • Cybersmile Foundation
