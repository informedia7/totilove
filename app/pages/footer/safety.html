Safety Tips

Your safety is our top priority. Follow these guidelines to stay protected online and in person.

Back to Home

Protect Your Personal Information

Never share your full name, home address, workplace, or financial details in early conversations. Use the in-app messaging system until you fully trust someone.

No home address • No bank details • No ID documents

Video Call Before Meeting

Before agreeing to a first in-person meeting, have a video call. This helps confirm the person is who they say they are and lets you get comfortable in a safe environment.

Safe First Meetings

  • Always meet in a public, populated place (café, restaurant, park).
  • Tell a friend or family member where you are going and who you are meeting.
  • Arrange your own transport — do not accept a ride from someone you just met.
  • Keep your phone charged and accessible at all times.

Recognise Red Flags

  • Requests for money or financial help (romance scams).
  • Refusing to video call or meet in person after prolonged chatting.
  • Stories that seem inconsistent or too perfect.
  • Excessive flattery or pressure to move the conversation off the platform.

Report & Block

If something feels wrong, trust your instincts. Use the Report button on any profile or message. Our moderation team reviews all reports within 24 hours. You can also Block any user instantly from their profile page.

Emergency Resources

If you are in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services. For online abuse support visit StopNCII.org or the Cybersmile Foundation.
